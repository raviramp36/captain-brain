2. TERMS OF CUSTODY

2.1 The Employee acknowledges receipt of the above assets and agrees to:

  • Use assets primarily for Company business purposes
  • Maintain assets in good working condition
  • Handle assets with reasonable care
  • Not lend, transfer, or allow unauthorized use of assets
  • Not modify, alter, or tamper with assets without authorization
  • Keep assets secure at all times
  • Follow all security and usage policies

2.2 Limited personal use of devices is permitted if it does not:

  • Interfere with work duties
  • Compromise security
  • Violate Company policies
  • Result in additional costs to the Company

3. CARE AND MAINTENANCE

3.1 The Employee shall:

  • Keep devices clean and protected from physical damage
  • Use protective cases/covers where provided
  • Not expose devices to extreme conditions (heat, water, dust)
  • Ensure proper charging practices (avoid overcharging)
  • Report malfunctions or issues promptly to IT
  • Not attempt unauthorized repairs or modifications
  • Keep software and security updates current

4. DAMAGE LIABILITY MATRIX

Damage Category Description Employee Liability
Normal Wear Gradual deterioration from regular use (minor scratches, battery degradation) 0% - No liability
Accidental (Care Taken) Unintentional damage despite reasonable care (spill with quick response, minor drop) 0-50% - Case by case
Negligent Damage Damage due to carelessness (leaving laptop unsecured, exposure to elements) 50-100% of repair/replacement
Rough Handling Damage from misuse (dropping, improper storage, ignoring warnings) 100% of repair/replacement
Intentional/Theft Deliberate damage, theft, or fraudulent loss claims 100% + Disciplinary action + Legal

5. LOSS OR THEFT

5.1 In case of loss or theft, the Employee must:

  • Report to IT and HR immediately (within 2 hours)
  • File a police complaint (FIR) within 24 hours
  • Provide a copy of FIR to the Company
  • Cooperate with any investigation

5.2 Liability for loss/theft:

  • Theft with FIR and due care proven: 0-50% liability (case by case)
  • Loss due to negligence: 50-100% liability
  • Failure to report promptly: 100% liability
  • Suspicious circumstances: Full investigation, potential 100% liability + action

6. RETURN OF ASSETS

6.1 The Employee shall return all assets to the Company:

  • Upon termination or resignation (on last working day)
  • Upon request by the Company at any time
  • Upon transfer to a different role/location if required
  • When assets are replaced or upgraded

6.2 Assets must be returned:

  • In working condition (normal wear accepted)
  • With all accessories and components
  • With all Company data backed up and personal data removed
  • With passwords/access credentials disclosed

6.3 Failure to return assets may result in:

  • Deduction of asset value from final settlement
  • Withholding of experience letter and relieving letter
  • Legal action for recovery

7. RECOVERY OF COSTS

7.1 Where the Employee is liable for damage, loss, or non-return:

  • The Company may deduct amounts from salary or final settlement
  • If deduction is insufficient, the Employee agrees to pay the balance
  • The Company may pursue legal recovery if necessary

7.2 The Employee hereby authorizes the Company to make such deductions as permitted by law.
