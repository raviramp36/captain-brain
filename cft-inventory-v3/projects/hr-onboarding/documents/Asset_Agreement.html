4. EMPLOYEE RESPONSIBILITIES

4.1 General Care & Handling

The Employee SHALL:

  1. Handle all assets with reasonable care as a prudent person would handle their own property;
  2. Keep assets clean, safe, and secure at all times;
  3. Use assets only for intended purposes;
  4. Store assets properly when not in use (e.g., laptop in bag, devices in secure location);
  5. Protect assets from theft, loss, damage, and environmental hazards (water, dust, extreme temperatures);
  6. Report any malfunction, damage, or issues immediately to IT/Admin;
  7. Follow manufacturer guidelines and Company IT policies.

4.2 Prohibited Actions

The Employee shall NOT:

  1. Handle assets roughly (throwing, dropping, slamming, etc.);
  2. Use excessive force on keyboards, mice, screens, or connectors;
  3. Eat or drink near laptops and keyboards;
  4. Expose devices to water, liquids, or moisture;
  5. Leave assets unattended in unsecured locations (public places, vehicles, etc.);
  6. Lend or transfer assets to any other person without authorization;
  7. Attempt to repair, modify, or disassemble assets without approval;
  8. Remove asset tags or identification stickers;
  9. Use assets for personal purposes beyond incidental use;
  10. Install unauthorized software or make unauthorized modifications;
  11. Use assets for any illegal or unauthorized activities.

4.3 Specific Care Instructions

💻 Laptop Care:

  • Always carry in a protective bag
  • Do not place heavy objects on top
  • Close lid gently, do not slam
  • Keep away from liquids and food
  • Do not block ventilation; use on hard surfaces
  • Shut down properly; do not force power off
  • Handle charging port and cables carefully

📱 Mobile Phone Care:

  • Use screen protector and protective case (if provided)
  • Do not expose to water or extreme temperatures
  • Handle charging port carefully
  • Do not overcharge or use non-approved chargers
  • Keep secure; do not leave unattended

⌨️ Keyboard & Mouse Care:

  • Do not press keys or buttons with excessive force
  • Keep clean and free from dust, crumbs, and spills
  • Do not throw or drop
  • Store properly when not in use
  • Handle cables carefully; do not pull or bend sharply

5. DAMAGE & LIABILITY

⚠️ DAMAGE LIABILITY POLICY

The Employee shall be financially liable for any damage to Company assets caused by:

  • Negligence - Failure to exercise reasonable care
  • Misuse - Using assets contrary to guidelines
  • Rough handling - Physical abuse of equipment
  • Unauthorized actions - Modifications, repairs, lending
  • Intentional damage - Willful destruction

5.1 Categories of Damage

Category Examples Employee Liability
Normal Wear & Tear Battery degradation, minor scratches from regular use, key fading No liability (Company bears cost)
Accidental Damage
(Genuine accident, reasonable care taken)		 Laptop fell from bag with broken strap, device stolen despite precautions Case-by-case review; partial liability may apply
Negligent Damage Liquid spill, left in unsecured location, dropped due to carelessness 50-100% of repair/replacement cost
Rough Handling Screen cracked from slamming lid, keys damaged from force, ports broken 100% of repair/replacement cost
Misuse/Unauthorized Use Personal modifications, unauthorized repairs, lending to others 100% of repair/replacement cost + disciplinary action
Intentional Damage Willful destruction, sabotage 100% cost + termination + legal action

5.2 Loss & Theft

Scenario Employee Liability
Theft with FIR filed, reasonable precautions taken No liability or partial liability (case-by-case)
Theft due to negligence (left in car, unattended in public) 50-100% of asset value
Loss without explanation 100% of asset value
Lost charger, cable, accessories 100% replacement cost

5.3 Recovery Process

If the Employee is liable for damage/loss:

  1. IT/Admin will assess the damage and determine repair/replacement cost;
  2. Employee will be informed of the liability amount;
  3. Employee may provide explanation or appeal within 3 working days;
  4. Final decision by Management/HR;
  5. Amount will be deducted from salary (monthly installments if amount is large);
  6. If employee exits before full recovery, balance will be deducted from final settlement.

6. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

The Employee MUST report to IT/Admin immediately (within 24 hours) in case of:

  1. Any damage to assets (even minor);
  2. Malfunction or technical issues;
  3. Loss or theft of any asset;
  4. Security incidents (hacking, virus, data breach);
  5. Accidental exposure to liquids or physical damage.

Failure to report promptly may result in increased liability.

7. MAINTENANCE & REPAIRS

  1. All repairs must be done through Company-authorized channels only;
  2. Employee shall NOT attempt self-repair or use unauthorized service centers;
  3. Routine maintenance (cleaning, software updates) may be required;
  4. Company will bear costs for repairs due to manufacturing defects or normal wear.

8. RETURN OF ASSETS

8.1 Upon Termination/Resignation

  1. All assets must be returned on or before the last working day;
  2. Assets must be returned in good working condition (fair wear accepted);
  3. All Company data must be backed up and handed over;
  4. Personal data must be removed by the Employee;
  5. Chargers, cables, bags, and all accessories must be returned;
  6. IT/Admin will inspect and document the condition.

8.2 Failure to Return

If Employee fails to return assets:

  1. Full asset value will be deducted from final settlement;
  2. Legal action may be initiated for recovery;
  3. Relieving letter may be withheld until assets are returned;
  4. Police complaint may be filed for misappropriation.
