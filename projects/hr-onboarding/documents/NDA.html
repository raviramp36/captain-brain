1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

1.1 "Confidential Information"

means all information, whether written, oral, electronic, visual, or in any other form, that is disclosed to or accessed by the Employee in connection with employment, including but not limited to:

1.2 Client & Business Information:
  1. Client names, contact details, and communication history;
  2. Client requirements, project specifications, and deliverables;
  3. Client contracts, proposals, and quotations;
  4. Pricing structures, discount policies, and commercial terms;
  5. Business relationships with clients, vendors, and partners.
1.3 Financial Information:
  1. Revenue, profit margins, and financial statements;
  2. Budgets, forecasts, and projections;
  3. Cost structures and pricing models;
  4. Investment details and funding information;
  5. Accounts receivable, payable, and cash flow data.
1.4 Salary & HR Information (STRICTLY CONFIDENTIAL):
  1. Own salary, compensation, and benefits details;
  2. Salary information of any other employee;
  3. Bonus structures, increments, and incentive plans;
  4. HR policies, appraisal records, and performance data;
  5. Employee personal information and records.

1.5 Technical & Operational Information:
  1. Proprietary technology, software, and source code;
  2. Product designs, specifications, and documentation;
  3. Manufacturing processes and methodologies;
  4. R&D activities, innovations, and patents;
  5. System architectures, databases, and infrastructure details;
  6. Internal tools, workflows, and operational procedures.
1.6 Strategic Information:
  1. Business strategies, plans, and roadmaps;
  2. Marketing strategies and campaign details;
  3. Expansion plans and new market initiatives;
  4. Competitive analysis and market intelligence;
  5. Partnership and acquisition discussions.
1.7 Third-Party Information:

Any information received from clients, vendors, or partners under confidentiality obligations.

2. OBLIGATIONS OF THE EMPLOYEE

2.1 Non-Disclosure

The Employee shall NOT:

  1. Disclose Confidential Information to any person, entity, or organization without prior written authorization;
  2. Discuss Confidential Information in public places, social gatherings, or online platforms;
  3. Share Confidential Information with family members, friends, or former colleagues;
  4. Disclose Confidential Information to competitors, whether directly or indirectly;
  5. Post or publish any Confidential Information on social media, websites, or forums.
2.2 Non-Use

The Employee shall NOT use Confidential Information for any purpose other than performing assigned duties, including:

  1. Personal financial gain or benefit;
  2. Starting or assisting a competing business;
  3. Helping any third party compete with the Company;
  4. Any purpose detrimental to the Company's interests.
2.3 Protection Measures

The Employee SHALL:

  1. Exercise at least the same degree of care to protect Confidential Information as for personal sensitive information;
  2. Keep all documents, files, and data secure and password-protected where applicable;
  3. Not copy, photograph, or record Confidential Information without authorization;
  4. Not store Confidential Information on personal devices without approval;
  5. Not forward Company emails or documents to personal email accounts;
  6. Immediately report any suspected or actual breach of confidentiality.
2.4 Need-to-Know Basis

The Employee shall only access and use Confidential Information that is necessary for performing assigned duties and shall not seek access to information beyond what is required.

3. EXCLUSIONS

The obligations of confidentiality shall NOT apply to information that:

  1. Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Employee;
  2. Was known to the Employee prior to disclosure, as evidenced by written records;
  3. Is independently developed by the Employee without use of Confidential Information;
  4. Is lawfully received from a third party without confidentiality restrictions;
  5. Is required to be disclosed by law, court order, or regulatory authority, provided the Employee gives prompt notice to the Company before such disclosure.

4. TERM AND SURVIVAL

4.1 During Employment

This Agreement is effective from the Effective Date and shall remain in force throughout the Employee's employment with the Company.

4.2 Post-Employment

The confidentiality obligations under this Agreement shall survive the termination or expiration of employment and continue for a period of THREE (3) YEARS after the last date of employment.

4.3 Trade Secrets

Notwithstanding the above, confidentiality obligations relating to trade secrets shall continue indefinitely for as long as such information remains a trade secret.

5. RETURN OF MATERIALS

Upon termination of employment or upon request by the Company, the Employee shall:

  1. Immediately return all documents, files, materials, and copies containing Confidential Information;
  2. Delete all Confidential Information from personal devices, cloud storage, and email accounts;
  3. Provide written confirmation of compliance with this clause;
  4. Not retain any copies, extracts, or summaries of Confidential Information.

6. REMEDIES

6.1 Breach Consequences

The Employee acknowledges that any breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to the Company for which monetary damages may be inadequate. In case of breach:

  1. The Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief without posting bond;
  2. The Company may pursue all available legal remedies, including damages;
  3. The Employee may face immediate termination of employment;
  4. The Company may recover all legal costs and expenses from the Employee;
  5. Criminal proceedings may be initiated under applicable laws.
6.2 Indemnification

The Employee shall indemnify and hold harmless the Company against any losses, damages, costs, or expenses arising from breach of this Agreement.
