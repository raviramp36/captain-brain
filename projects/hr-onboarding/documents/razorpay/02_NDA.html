1. PURPOSE

The Employee acknowledges that in the course of employment with the Company, the Employee will have access to and become acquainted with confidential and proprietary information belonging to the Company, its clients, vendors, and business partners. This Agreement sets forth the terms under which such information shall be protected.

2. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

2.1 "Confidential Information" means any and all information, in any form, that is not generally known to the public and that relates to the business of the Company, including but not limited to:

A. Client Information:

  • Client lists, contact details, and relationship history
  • Project specifications, requirements, and deliverables
  • Pricing proposals, contracts, and commercial terms
  • Client feedback, preferences, and strategic plans
  • Event details, guest lists, and venue information

B. Financial Information:

  • Revenue, profits, and financial statements
  • Pricing strategies and cost structures
  • Budget allocations and expenditures
  • Investment plans and funding details
  • Accounts receivable and payable information

C. Technical Information:

  • Source code, algorithms, and software designs
  • Hardware designs, schematics, and specifications
  • Product roadmaps and development plans
  • Manufacturing processes and techniques
  • Research and development projects

D. Business Information:

  • Business strategies, plans, and forecasts
  • Marketing strategies and campaigns
  • Vendor lists, contracts, and pricing
  • Partnership and collaboration agreements
  • Competitive analysis and market research

E. Human Resources Information:

  • Employee personal data and records
  • Salary, compensation, and benefits information
  • Performance evaluations and appraisals
  • Organizational structure and headcount
  • Recruitment strategies and candidate data

F. Trade Secrets:

  • Proprietary methodologies and processes
  • Unique techniques and innovations
  • Formulas, patterns, and compilations
  • Any information that derives economic value from being secret

3. OBLIGATIONS OF THE EMPLOYEE

3.1 The Employee agrees to:

A. Maintain Confidentiality:

  • Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence
  • Use at least the same degree of care to protect Confidential Information as the Employee uses to protect their own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care
  • Not disclose Confidential Information to any person, firm, or entity without prior written authorization from the Company

B. Restricted Use:

  • Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing job duties
  • Not use Confidential Information for personal benefit or the benefit of any third party
  • Not use Confidential Information to compete with the Company

C. No Unauthorized Copying:

  • Not copy, reproduce, or duplicate Confidential Information except as necessary for job duties
  • Not remove Confidential Information from Company premises without authorization
  • Not store Confidential Information on personal devices or accounts without approval

D. Security Measures:

  • Follow all Company information security policies and procedures
  • Protect passwords, access credentials, and authentication tokens
  • Report any suspected breach or unauthorized access immediately
  • Not share login credentials with any person

4. EXCLUSIONS

4.1 Confidential Information does not include information that:

  • Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Employee
  • Was in the Employee's possession prior to disclosure by the Company, without breach of any obligation
  • Is independently developed by the Employee without use of Confidential Information
  • Is disclosed with prior written approval from the Company
  • Is required to be disclosed by law, provided the Employee gives the Company prompt notice to seek protective measures

5. RETURN OF MATERIALS

5.1 Upon termination of employment or upon request by the Company, the Employee shall:

  • Immediately return all documents, materials, and property containing Confidential Information
  • Delete all Confidential Information from personal devices, accounts, and storage
  • Provide written certification of such return and deletion if requested
  • Not retain any copies, notes, or extracts of Confidential Information

6. DURATION OF OBLIGATIONS

6.1 The obligations under this Agreement shall:

  • Commence on the date of signing
  • Continue throughout the Employee's employment
  • Survive termination of employment for a period of THREE (3) YEARS
  • For trade secrets, continue indefinitely as long as the information remains a trade secret

7. THIRD-PARTY CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

7.1 The Employee acknowledges that the Company has obligations to protect confidential information of its clients, vendors, and partners.

7.2 The Employee agrees to treat third-party confidential information with the same level of protection as Company Confidential Information.

7.3 The Employee shall not bring any confidential information from previous employers or third parties to the Company.

8. REMEDIES

8.1 The Employee acknowledges that:

  • Breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to the Company
  • Monetary damages may be inadequate compensation for such breach
  • The Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief in addition to other remedies

8.2 In case of breach, the Company may:

  • Seek immediate injunctive relief
  • Claim damages for losses suffered
  • Terminate employment immediately
  • Pursue legal action including criminal proceedings where applicable
  • Recover legal costs and expenses

9. NO LICENSE

9.1 This Agreement does not grant the Employee any license or rights in or to the Confidential Information, except the limited right to use such information for performing job duties during employment.

10. GOVERNING LAW

10.1 This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of India.

10.2 Any disputes shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of courts in Bangalore, Karnataka.
