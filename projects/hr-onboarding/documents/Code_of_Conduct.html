Code of Conduct
Behavioral & Ethical Standards for All Employees
Effective Date: _____________ | Version 1.0
1. Introduction & Purpose

This Code of Conduct establishes the behavioral and ethical standards expected of all employees, contractors, and representatives of Craftech360 Private Limited ("Company"). It reflects our commitment to creating a professional, respectful, and legally compliant workplace.

Our Core Values:

🎯 Excellence 🤝 Integrity 💡 Innovation 🌟 Respect 🚀 Collaboration

This Code applies to all work-related activities, whether on Company premises, at client sites, during business travel, at company-sponsored events, or when representing the Company in any capacity.

2. Professional Conduct
2.1 Workplace Behavior

All employees shall:

  • Treat colleagues, clients, and partners with respect and dignity
  • Maintain professional demeanor in all interactions
  • Communicate clearly, honestly, and constructively
  • Take responsibility for their actions and decisions
  • Support team members and contribute positively to team dynamics
  • Dress appropriately for the work environment and client interactions
2.2 Attendance & Punctuality
  • Report to work on time as per assigned schedule
  • Notify supervisor promptly in case of absence or delay
  • Respect meeting times and deadlines
  • Complete assigned working hours faithfully
  • Request leave through proper channels in advance when possible
2.3 Quality & Performance
  • Deliver work that meets or exceeds quality standards
  • Meet deadlines and communicate proactively about challenges
  • Continuously improve skills and knowledge
  • Seek and provide constructive feedback
  • Take ownership of projects and see them through to completion
3. Prohibited Conduct
⚠️ Zero Tolerance Policy: The following behaviors will result in immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination without notice.
3.1 Harassment & Discrimination

The Company strictly prohibits any form of:

  • Sexual harassment — unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal/physical conduct of a sexual nature
  • Workplace bullying — repeated intimidation, humiliation, or offensive behavior
  • Discrimination — based on gender, religion, caste, race, ethnicity, age, disability, sexual orientation, or any protected characteristic
  • Retaliation — against anyone who reports violations or participates in investigations
3.2 Substance Abuse
  • Possession, use, or distribution of illegal drugs on Company premises or during work hours
  • Reporting to work under the influence of alcohol or drugs
  • Consumption of alcohol during work hours without explicit authorization
3.3 Violence & Threats
  • Physical violence or threats of violence
  • Possession of weapons on Company premises
  • Intimidating or threatening behavior
  • Damage to Company or personal property
3.4 Dishonesty & Fraud
  • Theft or misappropriation of Company assets
  • Falsification of records, time sheets, or expense reports
  • Providing false information during hiring or employment
  • Bribery or acceptance of improper payments
  • Forgery of signatures or documents
4. Confidentiality & Information Security

Employees must protect Company confidential information and that of our clients:

  • Never disclose proprietary information without authorization
  • Secure documents, devices, and access credentials
  • Follow information security policies and procedures
  • Report suspected data breaches immediately
  • Do not discuss confidential matters in public spaces
  • Return all confidential materials upon separation

Note: Detailed requirements are covered in the Information Security Policy and NDA.

5. Conflicts of Interest

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with Company interests:

  • Disclose any outside employment, business interests, or board positions
  • Do not engage in business relationships with competitors without approval
  • Avoid financial relationships with vendors, clients, or competitors that could influence decisions
  • Do not use Company resources for personal gain
  • Disclose family relationships with clients, vendors, or competitors
  • Seek approval before accepting gifts valued over ₹2,500
6. Company Resources & Assets

Company resources are provided for business purposes:

  • Use equipment, software, and materials responsibly and primarily for work
  • Limited personal use of Company systems is permitted if it does not interfere with work
  • Protect Company property from damage, theft, or misuse
  • Do not install unauthorized software or access unauthorized systems
  • Report equipment issues, damage, or loss promptly
  • Return all Company property upon request or separation
7. Social Media & External Communication
  • Do not speak on behalf of the Company without authorization
  • Clearly identify personal opinions as your own on social media
  • Do not share confidential Company information publicly
  • Do not post content that could damage the Company's reputation
  • Respect intellectual property rights in all posts
  • Direct media inquiries to authorized spokespersons
8. Client Relations

When interacting with clients:

  • Represent the Company professionally at all times
  • Deliver on commitments and communicate proactively
  • Maintain confidentiality of client information
  • Escalate issues appropriately rather than making unauthorized commitments
  • Treat client property and premises with respect
  • Adhere to client site rules and security protocols

10. Reporting Violations

  • Direct Supervisor — for general workplace issues
  • HR Department — hr@craftech360.com or +91 87920 14630
  • Management — for serious concerns or if other channels are unsuitable
  • Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) — for POSH-related complaints
