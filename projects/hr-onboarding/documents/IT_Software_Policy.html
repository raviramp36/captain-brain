IT & Software Usage Policy

Effective Date: _______________

1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes guidelines for the use of information technology resources, software, and digital services at Craftech360. It aims to:

  1. Ensure compliance with software licensing laws and intellectual property rights;
  2. Protect the Company from legal liability arising from unauthorized software use;
  3. Establish clear boundaries for use of company email and digital identity;
  4. Define employee responsibilities and accountability for IT resources.

2. SCOPE

This policy applies to:

  • All employees, interns, contractors, and consultants;
  • All devices owned, leased, or provided by the Company;
  • Personal devices used to access company resources or perform work;
  • All software, applications, and online services used for work purposes.

3. SOFTWARE LICENSING & USAGE

⚠️ STRICT PROHIBITION ON PIRATED/UNLICENSED SOFTWARE

The use of pirated, cracked, unlicensed, or illegally obtained software is STRICTLY PROHIBITED on any device used for work purposes, whether company-owned or personal.

3.1 Prohibited Software Activities

Employees shall NOT:

  1. Download, install, or use pirated software (cracked, keygen-activated, or torrented software);
  2. Use unlicensed copies of commercial software (including expired trials);
  3. Share software licenses beyond authorized users;
  4. Use personal software licenses for company work without approval;
  5. Install unauthorized software on company devices;
  6. Download software from untrusted sources (torrent sites, crack sites, etc.);
  7. Circumvent software protection mechanisms (DRM, license checks, etc.);
  8. Use company email to register for pirated or illegal software services.

3.2 Approved Software

Employees may only use:

  1. Software officially procured and licensed by the Company;
  2. Free/open-source software approved by IT/Management;
  3. Cloud services with valid company subscriptions;
  4. Trial versions with proper registration (for evaluation purposes only, with approval).

3.3 Software Request Process

If an employee requires software not currently available:

  1. Submit a request to the IT/Admin team or reporting manager;
  2. Provide justification for the business need;
  3. Wait for approval and official procurement;
  4. Do NOT install unauthorized alternatives in the meantime.

4. COMPANY EMAIL & DIGITAL IDENTITY

⚠️ CRITICAL: Company Email Usage Restrictions

Your company email address (@craftech360.com) represents the Company. Misuse can create legal liability for Craftech360.

4.1 Permitted Uses of Company Email

  1. Official business communication;
  2. Registration for work-related services approved by the Company;
  3. Professional networking (LinkedIn, industry forums);
  4. Communication with clients, vendors, and partners.

4.2 Prohibited Uses of Company Email

Employees shall NOT use company email to:

  1. Register for pirated or illegal software/services;
  2. Create accounts on unauthorized platforms;
  3. Sign up for personal subscriptions or services;
  4. Register on gambling, adult, or illegal websites;
  5. Create social media accounts for personal use;
  6. Sign up for services that may create liability for the Company;
  7. Participate in any activity that could bring disrepute to the Company.

🚨 WARNING: Legal Liability

If an employee uses their company email to register for pirated software or illegal services, and the Company receives legal notices, claims, or demands as a result:

  • The employee shall be personally liable for all claims, penalties, and damages;
  • The employee shall indemnify the Company against all costs and legal fees;
  • Such action constitutes gross misconduct and may result in immediate termination;
  • The Company may pursue legal action against the employee for recovery.

4.3 Separation of Personal and Professional Identity

Employees must:

  1. Use personal email for all personal registrations and services;
  2. Keep company email strictly for official purposes;
  3. Not link company email to personal financial accounts (UPI, banking, etc.);
  4. Inform IT immediately if company email is compromised or misused.

5. COMPANY DEVICES & EQUIPMENT

5.1 Use of Company Devices

Company-provided laptops, desktops, and mobile devices:

  1. Are for official business use only;
  2. Shall not be used for illegal activities;
  3. Shall not have unauthorized software installed;
  4. May be monitored by the Company;
  5. Must be returned upon termination.

5.2 Personal Devices (BYOD)

If using personal devices for work:

  1. The device must be free of pirated software;
  2. Company data must be kept secure;
  3. Employee remains liable for any illegal software on the device;
  4. Company may require audit/inspection if there are compliance concerns.

6. EMPLOYEE RESPONSIBILITIES

6.1 General Responsibilities

Every employee is responsible for:

  1. Understanding and complying with software licensing terms;
  2. Using only authorized and licensed software;
  3. Protecting company email and digital identity;
  4. Reporting any suspected licensing violations;
  5. Cooperating with audits and compliance checks.

6.2 Personal Liability

The employee acknowledges and agrees that:

  1. Any personal use of pirated software, even on personal devices, that creates liability for the Company shall be the sole responsibility of the employee;
  2. If the Company receives any legal notice, claim, demand, or penalty due to the employee's unauthorized software use, the employee shall bear all costs;
  3. The employee shall indemnify and defend the Company against any such claims;
  4. This liability survives termination of employment.

7. COMPLIANCE & MONITORING

7.1 Software Audits

The Company reserves the right to:

  1. Conduct periodic software audits on company devices;
  2. Request information about software installed on personal devices used for work;
  3. Remove unauthorized software from company devices;
  4. Investigate suspected violations.

7.2 Monitoring

Employees acknowledge that the Company may monitor:

  1. Software installations on company devices;
  2. Email usage and registrations;
  3. Internet browsing on company networks;
  4. Downloads and file transfers.

8. CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATION

Violation First Offense Repeat/Serious
Installing unauthorized software Written warning + removal Suspension/Termination
Using pirated software Final warning + liability acknowledgment Termination + legal action
Using company email for illegal services Termination Termination + recovery of damages
Causing legal liability to Company Termination + full indemnification Termination + legal action

9. INDEMNIFICATION

The employee hereby agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Craftech360 Private Limited, its Directors, officers, and employees from and against any and all:

  • Claims, demands, notices, or legal actions;
  • Penalties, fines, or license fees;
  • Damages, losses, and expenses;
  • Legal costs and attorney fees;

Arising from the employee's violation of this policy, including but not limited to use of pirated software, unauthorized registrations using company email, or any other breach that creates liability for the Company.

This indemnification obligation survives termination of employment.

10. REPORTING VIOLATIONS

Employees must report:

  1. Any suspected pirated software in the workplace;
  2. Any legal notices or demands received related to software;
  3. Any unauthorized use of company email or identity;
  4. Any security breaches or compromises.

Reports can be made to HR or Management. Good-faith reports will be protected.
