1. APPOINTMENT AND DUTIES

1.1 Position

The Company hereby employs the Employee in the position of _______________________ in the _______________________ Department, reporting to _______________________.

1.2 Duties and Responsibilities

The Employee shall:

  1. Perform all duties and responsibilities as assigned by the Company from time to time, consistent with the Employee's position;
  2. Devote full working time, attention, and abilities to the business and affairs of the Company;
  3. Faithfully and diligently perform assigned duties to the best of their abilities;
  4. Comply with all Company policies, procedures, rules, and regulations as amended from time to time;
  5. Act in the best interests of the Company at all times.
1.3 Place of Work

The Employee's primary place of work shall be the Company's office at _______________________________. The Company reserves the right to transfer the Employee to any other location, project site, or client premises as business requirements dictate.

2. TERM OF EMPLOYMENT

2.1 Commencement

The employment shall commence on _______________ (the "Start Date").

2.2 Probation Period

The Employee shall be on probation for a period of ____ months from the Start Date. During the probation period:

  1. Either Party may terminate this Agreement with 7 (seven) days' written notice or payment in lieu thereof;
  2. The Company shall evaluate the Employee's performance and suitability for the role;
  3. Upon successful completion of probation, the Employee shall be confirmed in writing;
  4. The Company may extend the probation period by up to 3 (three) months if required.
2.3 Confirmation

Upon successful completion of the probation period, the Employee shall be confirmed as a permanent employee of the Company, subject to continued satisfactory performance.

3. COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS

3.1 Salary

The Company shall pay the Employee a gross annual salary of ₹_______________ (Rupees _______________________________ only), payable monthly in arrears by the last working day of each calendar month. The salary structure is detailed in Schedule A attached hereto.

3.2 Deductions

The Company shall make deductions from the Employee's salary as required by applicable law, including but not limited to:

  1. Income Tax at Source (TDS);
  2. Provident Fund contributions;
  3. Professional Tax;
  4. Any other statutory deductions.
3.3 Benefits

The Employee shall be entitled to such benefits as may be provided by the Company from time to time, subject to applicable policies and eligibility criteria.

3.4 Salary Confidentiality

The Employee agrees that all compensation details are strictly confidential. The Employee shall not disclose salary information to any other employee, former employee, or third party. Violation of this clause shall be considered a serious breach of this Agreement.

4. WORKING HOURS AND LEAVE

4.1 Working Hours

The standard working hours shall be from 10:00 AM to 7:00 PM, Monday to Saturday, with a one-hour lunch break. The Employee may be required to work beyond standard hours or on holidays as per business requirements, without additional compensation unless specified otherwise.

4.2 Leave Entitlement

The Employee shall be entitled to leave as per the Company's Leave Policy, which may be amended from time to time. Leave must be applied for and approved in advance through the designated system.

4.3 Attendance

The Employee shall maintain regular attendance and punctuality. Unauthorized absence or habitual tardiness may result in disciplinary action.

5. CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means all information, whether written, oral, electronic, or visual, relating to the Company's business, including but not limited to:

  1. Trade secrets, proprietary information, and know-how;
  2. Client lists, contact details, project specifications, and pricing;
  3. Financial information, business plans, and strategies;
  4. Technical data, software, source code, and designs;
  5. Employee information, salary details, and HR records;
  6. Vendor and supplier information;
  7. Any information marked as "Confidential" or reasonably understood to be confidential.
5.2 Obligations

The Employee agrees to:

  1. Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;
  2. Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent;
  3. Use Confidential Information only for the purpose of performing duties;
  4. Take all reasonable measures to protect Confidential Information;
  5. Immediately report any unauthorized disclosure or breach.
5.3 Duration

The confidentiality obligations shall survive the termination of this Agreement and continue for a period of three (3) years thereafter, or indefinitely for trade secrets.

6. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

6.1 Work Product

All inventions, designs, software, code, documentation, creative works, and other intellectual property created by the Employee during employment, whether during working hours or otherwise, that relates to the Company's business shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Company ("Work Product").

6.2 Assignment

The Employee hereby irrevocably assigns to the Company all rights, title, and interest in and to all Work Product, including all intellectual property rights therein. The Employee agrees to execute any documents necessary to perfect such assignment.

6.3 Moral Rights

To the extent permitted by law, the Employee waives all moral rights in the Work Product.

7. NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

7.1 Non-Compete During Employment

During the term of employment, the Employee shall not, directly or indirectly:

  1. Engage in any business that competes with the Company;
  2. Provide services to any competitor of the Company;
  3. Hold any ownership interest in a competing business (except passive investments of less than 5% in publicly traded companies).
7.2 Non-Solicitation

During employment and for a period of twelve (12) months after termination, the Employee shall not:

  1. Solicit, induce, or attempt to induce any employee of the Company to leave their employment;
  2. Hire or engage any person who is or was an employee of the Company within the preceding 6 months;
  3. Solicit or attempt to solicit any client, customer, or business partner of the Company;
  4. Divert or attempt to divert any business opportunity from the Company.
7.3 Named Competitors

Without limiting the generality of the above, the following are identified as competitors: Jaseil Creations, 4Brains, Tagbin, MagicTap, InkinnCaps, Attah, Digital Jalebi, and any other entity engaged in experiential technology, kinetic installations, or event technology services.

8. SOFTWARE & IT USAGE

8.1 Prohibition on Pirated Software

The Employee shall NOT download, install, use, or possess any pirated, cracked, unlicensed, or illegally obtained software on any device used for work purposes, whether company-owned or personal. This includes software activated through keygens, cracks, or obtained from torrent/illegal sources.

8.2 Company Email Restrictions

The Employee's company email address (@craftech360.com) shall be used ONLY for official business purposes. The Employee shall NOT use company email to:

  1. Register for pirated software or illegal services;
  2. Create accounts on unauthorized platforms;
  3. Sign up for personal services or subscriptions;
  4. Engage in any activity that could create legal liability for the Company.
8.3 Personal Liability for Software Violations

The Employee acknowledges and agrees that:

  1. If the Employee uses pirated software (whether on company or personal devices) and the Company receives any legal notice, claim, demand, penalty, or enforcement action as a result, the Employee shall be solely and personally liable for all consequences;
  2. The Employee shall indemnify the Company and its Directors against all claims, penalties, fines, license fees, damages, legal costs, and expenses arising from such violations;
  3. Such violation constitutes gross misconduct and grounds for immediate termination;
  4. The Company may recover all costs from the Employee, including through legal proceedings;
  5. This liability survives termination of employment.
8.4 Software Compliance

The Employee shall:

  1. Use only software officially authorized and licensed by the Company;
  2. Request approval before installing any software on company devices;
  3. Cooperate with software audits and compliance checks;
  4. Report any suspected licensing violations immediately.

9. COMPANY PROPERTY AND ASSETS

8.1 Company Property

Any property provided to the Employee by the Company, including laptops, mobile phones, SIM cards, access cards, keys, documents, and equipment, shall remain the property of the Company and must be returned upon termination or upon request.

8.2 SIM Card Usage

If a Company SIM card registered under a Director's name is provided to the Employee, the Employee acknowledges that:

  1. The SIM card is Company property and for business use only;
  2. The Employee shall not use the SIM card for any illegal or unauthorized purposes;
  3. The Employee shall be fully liable for any misuse of the SIM card;
  4. The Employee shall indemnify the Company and its Directors against any claims arising from SIM card misuse;
  5. The SIM card must be returned upon termination.
8.3 Data Deletion

Upon termination, the Employee shall delete all Company data from personal devices and return all copies of Company documents and information.

9. TERMINATION

9.1 Termination by Notice

After confirmation, either Party may terminate this Agreement by giving thirty (30) days' written notice or payment/forfeiture of salary in lieu thereof. During the notice period:

  1. The Employee shall continue to perform duties diligently;
  2. The Employee shall complete proper handover of responsibilities;
  3. The Company may require the Employee to serve the full notice period or place them on garden leave.
9.2 Termination for Cause

The Company may terminate this Agreement immediately without notice or payment in lieu for cause, including but not limited to:

  1. Gross misconduct or willful neglect of duties;
  2. Breach of confidentiality or this Agreement;
  3. Fraud, dishonesty, or criminal activity;
  4. Insubordination or refusal to follow lawful instructions;
  5. Disclosure of salary or confidential information;
  6. Working for competitors or engaging in competing business;
  7. Habitual absenteeism or poor performance;
  8. Any act prejudicial to the interests of the Company.
9.3 Consequences of Termination

Upon termination for any reason:

  1. The Employee shall immediately return all Company property;
  2. The Employee shall complete all pending work and handover;
  3. The Company shall pay all dues owed up to the termination date;
  4. All confidentiality and non-solicitation obligations shall survive.

10. INDEMNIFICATION

The Employee agrees to indemnify and hold harmless the Company, its Directors, officers, and employees from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including legal fees) arising from:

  1. Breach of this Agreement by the Employee;
  2. Negligence or willful misconduct of the Employee;
  3. Violation of any law or third-party rights by the Employee;
  4. Misuse of Company property, including SIM cards.

11. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

11.1 Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India.

11.2 Jurisdiction

Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts in Bengaluru, Karnataka, India.

11.3 Arbitration

Before approaching courts, the Parties shall attempt to resolve any dispute through arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996. The arbitration shall be conducted in Bengaluru in English by a sole arbitrator mutually appointed by the Parties.

12. GENERAL PROVISIONS

12.1 Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with the Schedules and any policies referenced herein, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements.

12.2 Amendment

This Agreement may only be amended in writing signed by both Parties.

12.3 Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

12.4 Waiver

No waiver of any breach shall constitute a waiver of any other breach. No waiver shall be effective unless in writing.

12.5 Notices

All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered personally, by email, or by registered post to the addresses specified herein or as updated in writing.
